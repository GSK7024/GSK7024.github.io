Installation Guide: Optimizing Your Mesh Network

Placement is everything. Follow these rules for maximum speed:

  1. Centralize the Main Router: Don't hide your main router in a corner of the basement. Place it as centrally as possible, elevated off the floor.
  2. Line of Sight: Place your satellite nodes where they have a clear "line of sight" (or close to it) to the main router or another node. Thick concrete walls are Wi-Fi killers.
  3. The "Halfway" Rule: Place a satellite node halfway between the main router and the dead zone. If you put the node *in* the dead zone, it won't have a signal to boost!
  4. Wired Backhaul (Optional): For the absolute best performance, connect the nodes together using an Ethernet cable. This frees up the wireless bands for your devices.

Frequently Asked Questions

Mesh vs. Range Extender: What's the difference?

Range extenders just repeat a weak signal and often create a separate network name (SSID). Mesh systems create a single, seamless network that intelligently hands off your device to the closest node as you walk around.

Do I need Wi-Fi 6E?

If you buy a router in 2025, yes. It future-proofs your home. Even if you don't have 6E devices yet, the router uses the 6GHz band to communicate with itself, improving speed for everyone.

How many nodes do I need?

For a typical 2,500 sq ft home, 2 nodes (1 router + 1 satellite) are usually enough. For 3,000+ sq ft or multi-story homes, go for 3 nodes.
