Installation Guide: How to Wire Your Thermostat

Installing a smart thermostat is easier than you think. Follow these steps carefully:

  1. Turn Off Power: Go to your breaker box and turn off the power to your HVAC system (Furnace/AC). Verify it's off by trying to adjust your old thermostat.
  2. Take a Picture: Remove the faceplate of your old thermostat. Before disconnecting ANY wires, take a clear photo of how they are connected to the terminals (R, W, Y, G, C).
  3. Label Wires: Use the stickers provided in the box to label each wire as you disconnect it. Do not rely on wire color alone!
  4. Check for C-Wire: A "Common" (C) wire provides constant power to the smart thermostat. If you don't have one, use the Power Extender Kit (PEK) included with Ecobee, or a C-wire adapter.
  5. Mount Backplate: Screw the new backplate to the wall. Use the built-in level to ensure it's straight.
  6. Connect Wires: Insert the labeled wires into the corresponding terminals on the new backplate. Push the excess wire back into the wall.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need a C-wire?

For most smart thermostats, yes. Without a C-wire, the thermostat may steal power from the heating circuit, causing your furnace to short-cycle. Ecobee includes an adapter, while Nest can sometimes work without one (though we recommend it).

Nest vs. Ecobee: Which is better?

Choose Nest if you want simplicity and design. Choose Ecobee if you want data, remote sensors, and HomeKit integration.

Can I install this myself?

Absolutely. 90% of homeowners can install a smart thermostat in under 30 minutes with just a screwdriver and a smartphone.
